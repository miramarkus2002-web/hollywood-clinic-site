Twenty years ago, female hair loss was a quiet, rare concern. Today, it's one of the top three reasons women visit our clinic. So what changed?

## The Big Picture

Multiple environmental and lifestyle shifts are converging:

- **Hard water** in most Cairo districts
- **Heat styling** as a daily habit
- **Chronic stress** from urban life
- **Hormonal changes** from contraceptives and PCOS
- **Crash diets** that starve hair follicles

## The Three Main Patterns

### 1. Telogen Effluvium

The most common. Hair sheds 2–3 months after a stressful event — surgery, illness, childbirth, sudden weight loss, or emotional trauma. It usually resolves on its own within 6 months.

### 2. Female Pattern Hair Loss

Genetic. Begins as widening of the part line and thinning at the crown. Progressive without treatment.

### 3. Traction Alopecia

Caused by tight hairstyles — ponytails, braids, extensions worn for years. Reversible if caught early.

## Evidence-Based Treatments

At our clinic, we focus on what's clinically proven:

**Minoxidil 5%** — Topical solution, applied twice daily. Works for most patterns. First results in 3–4 months.

**PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)** — Your own blood, concentrated and injected into the scalp. Six monthly sessions for visible regrowth.

**Mesotherapy** — Vitamin and peptide cocktails delivered to the hair follicles.

**FUE Hair Transplant** — For advanced cases. Permanent, natural-looking results.

## What to Stop Doing Right Now

1. Heat styling above 180°C
2. Tight ponytails worn for hours
3. Chemical treatments stacked on top of each other (color + relaxer + keratin)
4. Skipping protein in meals
5. Ignoring it

## When to See a Specialist

If you're losing more than 100 hairs per day for more than two months, or seeing visible thinning, get evaluated. Hair restoration is dramatically more successful when caught early.
