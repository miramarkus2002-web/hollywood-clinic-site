## What Exosomes Actually Are

Exosomes are microscopic vesicles — tiny packages your body's cells use to send instructions to each other. Think of them as the postal service of your biology. When a healthy cell wants another cell to grow, repair, or behave a certain way, it sends an exosome carrying the message.

The exosomes we use in hair treatments are derived from advanced Korean stem-cell platforms. They arrive in our clinic in sterile, single-use vials and are packed with concentrated growth factors, peptides, and signaling molecules — the exact instructions a hair follicle needs to wake up, grow stronger hair, and resist the mechanisms of hair loss.

> "Growth factor injections feed the follicle. Exosomes reprogram it."
> — Dr. Rana Safwat, Dermatology & Aesthetics Consultant

## Why It Works for Hereditary Hair Loss

Most non-surgical hair treatments — minoxidil, traditional growth factors, PRP — work by nourishing or stimulating existing follicles. They help, but they don't address the underlying biological pattern causing hereditary hair loss.

Exosomes are different. They actively intervene in three ways:

1. **They extend the active growth phase** (anagen) of each hair, so hairs grow longer before shedding
2. **They reduce the immune-mediated attack** on follicles caused by DHT-related signaling
3. **They wake dormant follicles** that had stopped producing visible hair

This is why we recommend exosomes specifically for patients with hereditary patterns — male pattern baldness, female pattern thinning at the part line — where other treatments plateau.

## How Many Sessions Do You Need?

The standard protocol is **2–4 sessions, spaced 3–4 weeks apart**. Hair biology takes time, so:

- **Weeks 3–4:** shedding begins to slow
- **Weeks 8–12:** new growth becomes visible
- **Months 4–6:** density and quality improvements peak

After the initial course, most patients benefit from maintenance sessions every 6–12 months to preserve and extend results.

## The Authenticity Problem in Egypt

We need to be transparent about something. The Egyptian market is flooded with products labeled "exosomes" that aren't. Some are diluted preparations, others are mislabeled growth factors, and a few have completely unverified origins.

Authentic Korean exosomes are expensive because they're a real pharmaceutical product manufactured under strict cleanroom conditions, with documented purification, validated concentration, and traceable batch numbers. Heavily discounted "exosome" sessions almost certainly aren't using the real thing.

**Ask any clinic two questions before you book:**
1. Which Korean brand do you use?
2. Will you open the vial in front of me?

If they can't answer both with confidence, walk away.

## Who Should Consider Exosomes

Exosomes work especially well for:

- Men and women with hereditary pattern hair loss
- Patients with chronic shedding for more than 3 months
- Post-pregnancy women dealing with significant hair loss
- People who plateaued on minoxidil, finasteride, or PRP
- Anyone wanting to protect hair around a transplant

They are **not** a fit for cicatricial alopecia (where follicles are permanently destroyed) — exosomes can't regenerate dead follicles. For complete baldness, transplants remain the gold standard, and exosomes work brilliantly *around* them.
