What is Votiva?

Votiva is a non-surgical, doctor-led intimate wellness treatment, powered by InMode's FormaV and Morpheus8V technologies. Using gentle, controlled radiofrequency — and, where appropriate, fractional RF microneedling — Votiva improves the tone, comfort, moisture, and sensitivity of internal and external intimate tissue. It addresses concerns that commonly follow childbirth, ageing, and hormonal changes such as perimenopause and menopause: laxity, dryness, mild stress urinary incontinence, and reduced sensation. The treatment is comfortable, needs no anaesthesia and no downtime, and is performed entirely by certified female specialists in a completely private, confidential setting at Hollywood Clinic. Votiva is FDA-cleared, clinically studied, and suitable for all ages.

The Process

How the session works?

Private consultation — a confidential conversation with a female specialist to understand your concerns, history, and goals.

Personalised plan — a tailored protocol, typically three sessions about four weeks apart, explained fully so you know what to expect.

Comfort & privacy — you are treated in a dedicated private room by a female specialist, with full confidentiality at every step.

FormaV radiofrequency — a small applicator delivers gentle, uniform RF to warm the tissue and stimulate collagen, with real-time temperature control.

Morpheus8V (where indicated) — fractional RF microneedling may be added for deeper remodeling, based on your assessment.

What you feel — a comfortable warming sensation; the session usually takes around 30 minutes.

Right after — you return to daily activities the same day; your specialist advises a short interval before intimacy.

Results & follow-up — improvements build over about three months as collagen renews, with optional maintenance.

Contraindications

Safety first

Votiva is not suitable in the following cases. A confidential medical history and assessment is always taken before treatment at Hollywood Clinic.

  • Pregnancy or suspected pregnancy
  • Active urinary tract, vaginal, or pelvic infection
  • Active sexually transmitted infection
  • Recent gynaecological surgery or childbirth (a healing interval is required)
  • Active or recent cancer of the reproductive organs, or current radiotherapy to the area
  • Undiagnosed vaginal bleeding (requires medical evaluation first)
  • An implanted electrical device (e.g. pacemaker) or metal implant in the area — requires medical review for radiofrequency
  • Severe pelvic organ prolapse (may need a different, specialist approach)
  • Open wounds, active skin conditions, or lesions in the treatment area
  • A confidential medical history and assessment is always taken before Votiva at Hollywood Clinic

Treatment comparisons

Votiva vs. Other Options

Votiva vs. Surgery

Surgery (vaginoplasty/labiaplasty) is more aggressive, with incisions, anaesthesia, and real recovery. Votiva is non-surgical, comfortable, and needs no downtime — ideal for tone, comfort, and milder concerns rather than major structural change.

Votiva vs. Laser devices

Laser intimate devices ablate tissue with heat. Votiva uses gentler radiofrequency (and optional RF microneedling) with real-time temperature control, which many women find more comfortable, with strong collagen stimulation.

Votiva vs. Kegels

Pelvic floor exercises strengthen muscle and are valuable, but don’t restore tissue tone or moisture. Votiva works on the tissue itself and pairs well with pelvic floor training.

Votiva vs. Topicals

Topical estrogen and lubricants manage symptoms temporarily. Votiva treats the underlying tissue quality, with longer-lasting improvement in moisture and comfort.

Votiva vs. PRP

PRP (the "O-Shot") injects platelet-rich plasma to stimulate tissue. Votiva uses energy-based stimulation; the two can be complementary, and your specialist will advise what suits you.
