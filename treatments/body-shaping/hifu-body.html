About

What Is HIFU Body

HIFU Body is the body-specific version of HIFU (High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound) — the same technology used for non-surgical face lifting, but engineered with deeper applicators and higher-energy parameters for the thicker skin and larger surface areas of the body. HIFU works by delivering ultrasound energy in tiny, precise focal points beneath the skin's surface. The targeted tissue is heated to a temperature that triggers contraction of existing collagen and stimulates the production of new collagen and elastin. Because the energy bypasses the skin surface entirely, there's no damage to the outer layer — no peeling, no marks, no recovery time. Body HIFU applicators reach deeper than face applicators — typically 6mm, 9mm, and 13mm depths (versus 1.5mm, 3.0mm, and 4.5mm on the face). This is necessary because body skin is thicker and the structural layers requiring lifting are located deeper. The result is a gradual, natural-looking body skin tightening effect that develops over 2–3 months and continues to improve as new collagen forms.

Safety First

Contraindications

  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding
  • Pacemakers or implanted electronic medical devices
  • Severe or uncontrolled diabetes or impaired healing capacity
  • Active skin infection, severe acne, or open wounds in the treatment area
  • Severe body skin laxity that would only respond to surgery — HIFU Body has its limits; significant excess skin needs surgical removal (tummy tuck, arm lift)
  • Metal implants in the area being treated
  • Recent abdominal surgery (caesarean, tummy tuck, hernia repair) — wait for full healing
  • Permanent fillers or implants in the treatment area
  • Active cancer or radiation therapy in the area
  • Severe autoimmune skin conditions in the treatment area
  • Hernias in the treatment area
  • Bleeding or clotting disorders
  • A full medical history and skin assessment are always conducted before HIFU Body at Hollywood Clinic
  • Patients with very advanced sagging are honestly advised when surgical body procedures would be a better fit

Treatment Comparisons

HIFU Body vs. Other Treatments

HIFU Body vs. Other Body Treatments

HIFU Body vs. Morpheus8 Body Morpheus8 Body uses RF microneedling at the dermis level — best for stretch marks, skin texture, and body skin remodeling, with a few days of downtime. HIFU Body uses focused ultrasound to reach deeper tissue for non-surgical body lifting — stronger lifting effect, no downtime, but no stretch mark treatment. They're often combined for comprehensive body skin improvement.

HIFU Body vs. Body FX

Body FX uses surface RF + vacuum + electrical pulses for gentle fat reduction and skin tightening. HIFU Body works much deeper for stronger lifting and tightening. Different goals: Body FX for ongoing comfortable maintenance; HIFU Body for an annual deep lift.

HIFU Body vs. BodyTite

BodyTite is a minimally invasive procedure (tiny incision, local anesthesia) that removes fat AND tightens skin in one procedure — strong results in one session with a few days of downtime. HIFU Body is fully non-invasive with no downtime but doesn't remove any fat. For patients with both fat and loose skin, BodyTite often delivers a more comprehensive result; for those with only loose skin (no excess fat), HIFU Body alone may be ideal.

HIFU Body vs. Tummy Tuck (Abdominoplasty)

A tummy tuck removes excess skin completely and tightens abdominal muscles — needed for patients with substantial skin redundancy or post-pregnancy muscle separation. HIFU Body tightens existing skin but cannot remove excess skin. For mild post-pregnancy abdomen, HIFU Body is an excellent non-surgical option; for severe cases, surgery is still the right answer.

HIFU Body vs. Tesla Former / EMSculpt

Tesla Former builds muscle. HIFU Body tightens skin. Completely different layers and goals — often combined: Tesla Former for muscle, HIFU Body for skin on top.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Does HIFU Body hurt?
Body areas typically have less concentrated nerve sensitivity than the face, so HIFU Body is usually better tolerated than face HIFU. You'll feel warm pulses during energy delivery — uncomfortable for some patients but well within normal tolerance for most. Topical numbing can be used for sensitive cases.
How long does a HIFU Body session take?
Typically 45–90 minutes depending on the size of the treatment area and the protocol depth used.
Is there any downtime?
No. There may be mild redness or temporary tingling immediately after the session, but no peeling, marks, or recovery time. You can return to normal activities the same day.
When will I see results?
Some patients notice subtle changes within days, but the major lifting effect develops over 2–3 months as new collagen forms — and continues improving for up to 6 months.
How long do HIFU Body results last?
Typically 12–18 months. Most patients schedule a HIFU Body session once a year per area to maintain results.
Is HIFU Body safe for darker skin?
Yes — completely. HIFU uses ultrasound, which doesn't target melanin, so there's no risk of pigmentation. It's safe for all Fitzpatrick skin types.
Can HIFU Body replace a tummy tuck?
Not exactly. A surgical tummy tuck remains the gold standard for very advanced abdominal skin laxity, excess skin removal, and muscle separation repair. HIFU Body offers a meaningful non-surgical alternative for patients with mild to moderate post-pregnancy abdominal laxity — or for those wanting to delay surgery.
Will HIFU Body help with stretch marks?
HIFU Body is not the primary treatment for stretch marks — it tightens skin but doesn't directly remodel stretch mark texture. For stretch marks specifically, Morpheus8 Body is the better choice. Combining the two often produces the best result: Morpheus8 Body for the stretch marks, HIFU Body for the overall skin tightening.
How is HIFU Body different from HIFU Face?
Same technology, deeper depths for the body (6/9/13mm vs 1.5/3.0/4.5mm for face). The face targets the SMAS layer specifically; the body targets deeper fascial layers. Different applicators, different protocols — both produce non-surgical lifting via collagen stimulation. How can I tell if I'**
