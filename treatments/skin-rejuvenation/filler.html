About

What are Dermal Fillers

Dermal fillers are injectable gels — most often based on hyaluronic acid (HA), a substance the body produces naturally — that restore volume, define features, and lift facial structures without surgery. With age, weight changes, or genetics, certain areas of the face lose support and volume; fillers replace it in a precise, controlled way. At Hollywood Clinic Egypt, fillers are placed by certified injectors using only internationally approved brands. Every treatment starts with a full facial assessment — because beautiful filler work is about facial harmony, not just adding volume in one spot. Filler can be done on virtually every facial zone: lips, cheeks, mid-face, under-eye, nose (non-surgical rhinoplasty), chin, jawline, temples, and even hands and the body. Each area requires a different filler density and injection technique.

Post-treatment Guidelines

  • Mild swelling and slight bruising are normal for 24–72 hours
  • Avoid touching, massaging, or applying pressure on the treated area for 24 hours
  • Avoid intense exercise, saunas, and steam rooms for 48 hours
  • Sleep on your back if possible for the first 1–2 nights
  • Avoid dental work for 2 weeks after lip or cheek filler

Safety First

Contraindications

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women
  • Patients with active skin infections or inflammation at the injection site
  • Anyone with a known allergy to hyaluronic acid or lidocaine (in fillers that contain it)
  • Patients with autoimmune disorders — requires careful medical evaluation
  • Patients on blood thinners or with bleeding disorders — requires medical clearance
  • Anyone with a history of severe allergic reactions or anaphylaxis
  • Patients with recent dental work, vaccinations, or scheduled major surgery — timing should be discussed
  • History of cold sores around the injection area — antiviral prophylaxis may be needed
  • Body Dysmorphic Disorder (BDD) or unrealistic expectations — a careful conversation is essential
  • Warning about permanent fillers: Hollywood Clinic uses only reversible hyaluronic acid fillers
  • Permanent fillers (silicone, polyacrylamide) are not recommended for the face due to long-term complications — avoid them regardless of cost

Treatment Comparisons

Dermal Fillers vs. Other Treatments

Filler vs. Botox

Filler adds volume and reshapes; Botox relaxes muscles to smooth expression lines. They treat different things and are often combined ('liquid facelift').

Filler vs. Skin Boosters

Filler reshapes; Skin Boosters don't reshape — they improve skin quality from within (hydration, glow, elasticity). Different purpose, often combined.

Filler vs. Biostimulators

Filler gives immediate volume by physically filling the area. Biostimulators trigger your body to gradually build its own collagen over months. Filler = instant; biostimulators = gradual but longer-lasting.

Filler vs. Fat Transfer

Fat transfer is a surgical procedure that moves your own fat. Filler is a 30-minute non-surgical session. Filler is reversible; fat transfer is not, and results are less predictable.

Filler vs. Surgical Facelift

Filler restores volume to lift the face without cutting. Facelift is surgery that removes excess skin. For early-to-moderate aging, filler often achieves what patients want — without the OR.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Are fillers safe?
Yes — when performed by a qualified injector using FDA/CE-approved hyaluronic acid fillers. Hyaluronic acid is naturally found in the body, and HA fillers have decades of safety data.
Do fillers hurt?
Most fillers contain built-in lidocaine (anesthetic), and topical numbing cream is also applied. Most patients describe the discomfort as mild — quick pinches rather than real pain.
How long do filler results last?
Depending on the filler type and area: lips 6–12 months, cheeks and jawline 12–18 months, under-eye 9–12 months. Areas with more movement (like lips) break down faster.
Can fillers be removed?
Yes — hyaluronic acid fillers can be dissolved with an enzyme called hyaluronidase if you're unhappy with the result or if there's a complication. This is one of the biggest safety advantages of HA fillers.
Will I look 'overdone' or fake?
Not when filler is placed correctly by a skilled injector who respects your anatomy. Hollywood Clinic's philosophy is enhancement, not transformation — you should look like a refreshed version of yourself.
Is there downtime?
Minimal. You can return to normal activities immediately, but expect some mild swelling for 24–72 hours and possibly small bruises. Most patients book filler 1–2 weeks before a major event.
How do I know my filler is real?
At Hollywood Clinic, the sealed syringe is shown to you before injection so you can verify the brand, batch number, and expiration date. This is standard practice in any reputable clinic.
Can I combine filler with Botox?
Yes — this combination is commonly called a 'liquid facelift' and addresses both volume loss and expression lines for a complete rejuvenation result.
