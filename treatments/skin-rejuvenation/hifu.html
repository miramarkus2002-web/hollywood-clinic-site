What is HIFU?

HIFU (High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound) is a non-surgical face and neck lifting treatment that uses focused ultrasound energy to reach deeper layers of the skin than any other non-invasive technology can — specifically, the SMAS layer (Superficial Musculoaponeurotic System). This is the exact same layer that plastic surgeons tighten and reposition during a surgical face lift. HIFU delivers ultrasound energy in tiny, precise focal points beneath the skin's surface, heating the targeted tissue to a temperature that triggers contraction of existing collagen and stimulates the production of new collagen and elastin. Because the energy bypasses the surface of the skin entirely, there's no damage to the outer layer — no peeling, no marks, no recovery. The result is a gradual, natural-looking lift and tightening effect that develops over 2–3 months and continues to improve as new collagen forms. HIFU is FDA-approved and considered one of the most effective non-surgical lifting technologies available in the world today.

The Process

How the session works?

Consultation & assessment — your doctor evaluates your skin laxity and goals and confirms HIFU is suitable for the lift you want.

Cleansing & marking — the skin is cleansed and a treatment grid marked for even, precise delivery.

Depth selection — the right cartridges and depths are chosen to target the correct layers, including the SMAS.

Focused ultrasound — energy is delivered to tiny focal points beneath the skin, heating tissue to the collagen-stimulating temperature without harming the surface.

What you feel — brief warm or tingling pulses deep in the skin; comfort settings are adjusted as needed.

Even coverage — the doctor works methodically across the grid so the whole area is treated.

Right after — mild redness or slight tenderness may appear briefly; you return to normal life immediately.

Results & plan — some lift is visible early, building over 2–3 months as collagen forms. Most people need one session, reviewed at follow-up.

Contraindications

Safety first

HIFU is not suitable in the following cases. A full medical assessment is always done before treatment at Hollywood Clinic.

  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding
  • Pacemakers or implanted electronic medical devices
  • Severe or uncontrolled diabetes or impaired healing capacity
  • Active skin infection, severe acne, or open wounds in the treatment area
  • Active herpes or cold sore outbreak
  • Severe skin laxity that would only respond to surgery — HIFU has its limits; significant sagging needs a surgical lift
  • Metal implants in the area being treated
  • Permanent fillers in the treatment area
  • Recent injectable treatments — typically a 2-week separation is recommended
  • Active cancer or radiation therapy in the area
  • Severe autoimmune skin conditions in the treatment area
  • A full medical history and skin assessment are always conducted before HIFU at Hollywood Clinic
  • Patients with very advanced sagging are honestly advised when surgical options would be a better fit

Treatment comparisons

HIFU vs. Other Treatments

HIFU vs. Surgical Face Lift

A surgical face lift remains the gold standard for dramatic, long-lasting lifting (10+ years) — but with general anesthesia, scars, recovery, and significant cost. HIFU delivers a meaningful percentage of that lifting effect without any of the surgical risks, ideal for patients who aren't ready for surgery or want to delay it.

HIFU vs. Thread Lift

Thread lifts physically lift sagging tissue using dissolvable threads inserted under the skin — strong immediate lift, but the threads dissolve in 12–18 months. HIFU also lasts 12–18 months but stimulates the body's own collagen without inserting any foreign material. Many patients combine the two: threads for immediate lift, HIFU for collagen support.

HIFU vs. Forma (Radiofrequency Tightening)

Forma is more comfortable, more gradual, and works on skin tightening without the deeper SMAS lift. HIFU is stronger, deeper, and more lifting-focused — but can feel slightly uncomfortable during the session. They're often used in combination: Forma for ongoing maintenance and surface refinement, HIFU once a year for the deep lift.

HIFU vs. Morpheus8

Morpheus8 uses RF microneedling for collagen remodeling and addresses acne scars exceptionally well, with mild downtime. HIFU is non-invasive with zero downtime and focuses on lifting rather than scar treatment. Many comprehensive plans use both — Morpheus8 for texture and scars, HIFU for lifting.

HIFU vs. Biostimulators (Sculptra, Radiesse, Ellansé)

Biostimulators are injected and stimulate collagen from inside the tissue — strong, long-lasting volumization and firmness. HIFU is external and produces lifting through ultrasound. The two approaches complement each other: biostimulators restore volume and structure, HIFU lifts the supporting layer.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Does HIFU hurt?

Mild, brief discomfort during energy delivery is normal — most patients describe it as quick warm pulses. It's well-tolerated by the majority of patients, and topical numbing can be used for sensitive cases.

How long does a HIFU session take?

Typically 45–90 minutes depending on the size of the treatment area and the protocol depth used.

Is there any downtime?

No. There may be mild redness or temporary tingling immediately after the session, but no peeling, marks, or recovery time. You can return to normal activities the same day.

When will I see results?

Some patients notice subtle changes within days, but the major lifting effect develops over 2–3 months as new collagen forms — and continues improving for up to 6 months.

How long do HIFU results last?

Typically 12–18 months. Most patients schedule a HIFU session once a year to maintain results.

Is HIFU safe for darker skin tones?

Yes — completely. HIFU uses ultrasound, which doesn't target melanin in the skin, so there's no risk of pigmentation. It's safe for all Fitzpatrick skin types.

Can HIFU replace a surgical face lift?

Not exactly. A surgical face lift remains the gold standard for very advanced sagging. HIFU offers a meaningful non-surgical alternative for patients with mild to moderate laxity — or for those wanting to delay surgery.

How can I tell if I'm getting a real HIFU session?

Real medical HIFU devices deliver focused ultrasound with measurable energy points, three-depth precision, and visible markings on the screen during treatment. Authentic devices include Ultraformer, Ultherapy, and similar FDA-approved platforms. Always ask the clinic about the device. Hollywood Clinic uses only authentic medical-grade HIFU technology.

How is HIFU different from Forma?

HIFU uses focused ultrasound to reach the deep SMAS layer for lifting. Forma uses radiofrequency at shallower depths for skin tightening and is more comfortable. HIFU is more powerful for lifting; Forma is gentler for maintenance. Many patients use both.
