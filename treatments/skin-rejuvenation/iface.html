About

What Is iFace

iFace is Hollywood Clinic's signature combined facial treatment that pairs two of the most respected medical aesthetic procedures in the world — Hydrafacial and Forma — into a single visit, delivering both surface-level transformation and deeper skin tightening in one session. The combination is intentional: Hydrafacial cleanses, exfoliates, hydrates, and infuses customized serums into the skin, while Forma uses controlled radiofrequency to warm deeper layers and stimulate collagen and elastin — tightening the skin from within. The result is something a single treatment can't deliver alone: immediately brighter, deeply cleansed, perfectly hydrated skin on the outside, combined with firmer, more lifted, more youthful skin on the inside. All in one no-downtime visit.

Safety First

Contraindications

  • Since iFace combines two treatments, the contraindications of both apply: Pregnancy and breastfeeding (some serums and the radiofrequency component are not advised)
  • Pacemakers, implanted defibrillators, or other implanted electronic medical devices
  • Active severe acne, cystic lesions, or open wounds in the treatment area
  • Active herpes or cold sore outbreak
  • Severe rosacea flare-ups or sunburn at the time of the session
  • Permanent fillers or unidentified implants in the treatment area
  • Recent isotretinoin (Accutane) use — typically a 6-month wait is recommended
  • Active cancer or radiation therapy in the area
  • Known allergy to any of the serum ingredients
  • A complete medical and skin assessment is always conducted before iFace at Hollywood Clinic to make sure the protocol is fully safe for you

Treatment Comparisons

iFace vs. Other Treatments

iFace vs. Hydrafacial alone

Hydrafacial alone gives you cleaner, brighter, more hydrated skin — but it doesn't tighten or stimulate collagen in the deeper layers. iFace adds Forma's collagen-stimulating tightening on top of all the Hydrafacial benefits, in the same visit.

iFace vs. Forma alone

Forma alone gives you collagen stimulation and gradual tightening — but it doesn't deep-cleanse the pores or deliver hydrating serums. iFace adds Hydrafacial's surface transformation on top of the Forma lift.

iFace vs. Booking Hydrafacial + Forma separately

Doing them separately is functionally similar — but it costs two visits and two appointments. iFace is the optimized, time-efficient combined protocol with package pricing.

iFace vs. Morpheus8

Morpheus8 is more aggressive — it uses microneedling with RF for deeper collagen remodeling and can address acne scars. It has some downtime (a few days of redness/marks). iFace is fully non-invasive with zero downtime and is best for patients who want lifting and glow without any recovery time.

iFace vs. HIFU

HIFU produces a stronger, more dramatic lifting result by targeting the SMAS layer ultrasonically. iFace is gentler, more comfortable, and ideal for ongoing maintenance and refinement — especially around delicate areas like the eyes. Many patients combine HIFU once or twice a year with iFace sessions in between.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between iFace and a regular Hydrafacial?
A regular Hydrafacial gives you surface results — glow, hydration, deep cleansing. iFace adds Forma's deep radiofrequency skin tightening on top, in the same session — so you get both surface transformation and deeper lifting in one visit.
How long does an iFace session take?
Around 60–75 minutes, depending on the area treated and the protocol customization.
Is there any downtime?
None at all. iFace is fully non-invasive — no redness that lasts, no peeling, no marks. You can return to work, exercise, or attend an event immediately after the session.
Will I see results after the first session?
Yes. The Hydrafacial component gives an immediate glow and hydration. The Forma component delivers a subtle tightening right away, with deeper, longer-lasting collagen-based improvement developing over several weeks.
How often should I do iFace?
This varies based on your skin and goals. Many patients do an initial series for maximum effect and then move to maintenance sessions. Your doctor will design a schedule for you.
Is iFace better than doing Hydrafacial and Forma separately?
Practically yes — same effect, but in one optimized visit at a better package rate. The Hollywood Clinic protocol also orders the steps for maximum synergy: Hydrafacial first to clean the skin, then Forma to penetrate freshly prepped skin.
Is iFace safe for all skin tones?
Yes. Both Hydrafacial and Forma are safe across all skin types and tones. There is no pigmentation risk.
Can iFace be combined with other treatments?
Yes. iFace pairs well with injectables (Botox, fillers, skin boosters, biostimulators) and even Morpheus8 in a structured plan — but the doctor will decide the best timing for safety and results.
