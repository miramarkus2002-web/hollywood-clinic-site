About

What Is Growth Factor for Hair?

Growth Factor for hair is a concentrated multivitamin and peptide injection treatment delivered directly into the scalp. The formulation is a carefully balanced blend of vitamins, amino acids, minerals, peptides, and biological growth-stimulating compounds — designed to deeply nourish the hair follicles, prevent shedding, stimulate new growth, and restore healthy hair quality. The treatment works on a simple principle: hair follicles are biological structures that need a specific cocktail of nutrients to function optimally. When the follicle is undernourished — whether from stress, hormonal imbalance, deficiencies, or aging — hairs grow thinner, shorter, and shed faster. Growth Factor injections deliver these nutrients directly where they're needed, bypassing the digestive system entirely for maximum bioavailability. Unlike oral hair vitamins (which lose most of their potency in digestion) or topical hair products (which barely penetrate the scalp surface), Growth Factor injections place the active ingredients exactly at the level of the hair follicle root — where they can actually work.

The Process

How the Session Works

1

Consultation & Scalp Assessment

Your doctor evaluates your scalp, identifies areas of thinning and shedding, and confirms Growth Factor injections are right for your case. In some cases, lab tests for hair-related deficiencies (iron, ferritin, vitamin D, thyroid) may be recommended.

2

Scalp Preparation

The scalp is cleansed. A topical numbing cream may be applied to make the injections more comfortable.

3

Growth Factor Preparation

The Growth Factor solution — a sterile, pre-mixed pharmaceutical-grade formulation of vitamins, peptides, and growth-stimulating compounds — is prepared in front of you.

4

Scalp Injection

The solution is injected into the scalp using fine needles in a systematic grid pattern across the target areas. Each injection is small and quick. Most patients describe the sensation as brief pricks.

5

Post-Treatment Care

No washing, no swimming, no heavy sweating for 24 hours. You can return to work or daily activities immediately. There may be slight redness or tiny dot-like marks for a few hours, but no real downtime.

6

Session Schedule

A standard Growth Factor course is typically 4–6 sessions, spaced 2–3 weeks apart for the initial phase. Most patients then continue with maintenance sessions every 1–3 months depending on hair condition and goals.

Safety First

Contraindications

  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding (some vitamin combinations are not advised)
  • Active scalp infection, severe dermatitis, or open wounds in the treatment area
  • Active herpes outbreak on the scalp
  • Severe uncontrolled diabetes or impaired healing capacity
  • Bleeding or clotting disorders, or current use of strong blood thinners — requires medical review
  • Severe autoimmune conditions affecting the scalp
  • Known allergy to any of the vitamin or peptide components in the formulation — disclose all allergies during consultation
  • Fever or acute illness at the time of the session
  • Active chemotherapy or recent radiation — wait until cleared by your oncologist
  • Cicatricial (scarring) alopecia where follicles are permanently destroyed — Growth Factor won't regenerate dead follicles
  • A full medical history and scalp assessment are always conducted before Growth Factor at Hollywood Clinic. If your hair loss has a hormonal, nutritional, or thyroid root cause, the doctor may recommend lab tests and addressing that cause alongside the treatment.

Treatment Comparisons

Growth Factor vs. Other Hair Loss Treatments

Growth Factor vs. Exosomes

Growth Factor delivers concentrated vitamins, amino acids, and growth-stimulating compounds — feeding and supporting the follicle. Exosomes deliver cellular signaling molecules that change how the follicle actually behaves at a programming level. Growth Factor feeds; Exosomes reprogram. They are complementary — many patients combine both, with Exosomes being the stronger transformational treatment and Growth Factor being the excellent supportive and maintenance treatment.

Growth Factor vs. PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)

PRP uses your own blood, concentrated for growth factors and platelets — natural but variable depending on your blood quality. Growth Factor uses a standardized pharmaceutical formulation — consistent and predictable. PRP can be more powerful biologically for some patients; Growth Factor is more consistent across different patients. Many doctors use both at different stages.

Growth Factor vs. Mesotherapy for Hair

These two terms are often used interchangeably in the market — both refer to scalp injections of a vitamin-rich solution. Strictly speaking, Mesotherapy is the technique (small superficial injections in a grid pattern), while Growth Factor refers to a specific category of formulation that's typically more concentrated and contains additional peptide/signaling compounds beyond basic vitamins.

Growth Factor vs. Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a topical drug applied daily — it extends the active growth phase of hair. Growth Factor is an injection treatment performed periodically — it provides comprehensive nutritional support and partial growth stimulation. They work through different mechanisms and are often used together: Minoxidil daily, Growth Factor every 2–3 weeks.

Growth Factor vs. Hair Transplant

Hair transplant surgically moves follicles from a healthy donor area to bald zones — permanent and dramatic for advanced cases. Growth Factor strengthens existing follicles but doesn't add new ones. Best combination: hair transplant for advanced baldness; Growth Factor before, around, and after the transplant to support healthy hair throughout the scalp.

Growth Factor vs. Oral Hair Vitamins

Oral hair supplements (biotin, B-complex, etc.) help when there's a true nutritional deficiency — but most of the dose is lost in digestion, and they cannot deliver targeted concentration to the scalp. Growth Factor injections place the same active ingredients directly at the follicle root — far more efficient.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Growth Factor treatment hurt?
Mild discomfort during injections — most patients describe it as small, brief pricks. Topical numbing cream can be applied before the session to make it more comfortable. After the session there's no real pain.
How many sessions do I need?
A typical Growth Factor course is 4–6 sessions, spaced 2–3 weeks apart for the initial phase. After that, maintenance sessions every 1–3 months are recommended depending on your hair condition. Your doctor will design a schedule for your specific case.
When will I see results?
Hair shedding usually reduces within 3–4 weeks. New hair growth and improved density become visible after 6–8 weeks and continue developing over 3–4 months. Hair biology takes time — patience is essential.
Are the Growth Factor injections safe?
Yes — authentic pharmaceutical-grade formulations are very safe when administered by a qualified medical professional. Side effects are mild and temporary (slight redness, brief tenderness, tiny pinpoint marks for a few hours). Hollywood Clinic uses only sterile, single-use materials.
Will Growth Factor work for hereditary baldness?
Growth Factor helps slow down hair loss in hereditary cases and strengthens existing hair, but it's not the most powerful treatment for advanced hereditary baldness on its own. For hereditary hair loss specifically, Exosomes are typically more powerful — and many doctors recommend combining the two, or combining Growth Factor with Minoxidil and Finasteride, depending on the case.
How long do results last?
With a complete course plus maintenance sessions every 1–3 months, results can be sustained long-term. Without maintenance, the underlying causes of hair loss will gradually return — Growth Factor doesn't permanently change the biology, it provides ongoing support.
Is there any downtime?
No real downtime. You can return to normal activities immediately. We ask you to avoid washing your hair, swimming, or heavy sweating for 24 hours after the session for optimal absorption.
Can I combine Growth Factor with other hair treatments?
Yes — Growth Factor pairs excellently with Exosomes, PRP, Minoxidil, Finasteride, and hair transplants. It's often used as part of a multi-treatment plan and is especially valuable as ongoing maintenance between more advanced therapies.
Is it the same as Mesotherapy for hair?
In everyday market use, the two terms overlap significantly. Strictly, Mesotherapy is the injection technique, while Growth Factor refers to a specific category of formulation (typically more concentrated, with peptide/signaling compounds in addition to vitamins). The line between the two has blurred in clinical practice — what matters is the quality and authenticity of the formulation being used.
